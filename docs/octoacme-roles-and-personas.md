# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## QA/Testing Lead

### Role Summary
QA/Testing Leads design and oversee quality assurance strategies, test planning, and acceptance validation. They ensure features meet acceptance criteria and quality standards before release.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain test plans and acceptance criteria
- Design and execute manual and automated testing
- Identify and track quality issues and regressions
- Collaborate with developers on testability and definition of done
- Conduct smoke tests and UAT coordination

### Goals
- Ensure zero critical bugs reach production
- Reduce time spent on regression testing through automation
- Validate customer-facing quality and usability

### Typical Communication
- Sprint planning and backlog refinement sessions
- Daily standup updates on test progress and blockers
- Release sign-off and acceptance criteria validation

### Interaction with Other Roles
- Works closely with **Developers** to define testability requirements and acceptance criteria
- Partners with **Product Managers** to clarify acceptance criteria and success metrics
- Collaborates with **Project Managers** on test timelines and release readiness
- Reports testing progress and quality risks to the team and stakeholders

---

## Technical Architect

### Role Summary
Technical Architects define system design, technical strategy, and scalability approaches. They guide architectural decisions and ensure alignment with long-term technical vision.

### Responsibilities
- Evaluate technical trade-offs and propose architecture solutions
- Review and approve system design for new features
- Identify technical risks and propose mitigations
- Mentor developers on architectural patterns and best practices
- Ensure compliance with technical standards and security guidelines

### Goals
- Deliver scalable, maintainable systems
- Reduce technical debt and rework
- Enable long-term product extensibility

### Typical Communication
- Design review meetings and technical assessments
- Architecture decision records (ADRs)
- Monthly alignment with Product and Project Managers on technical roadmap

### Interaction with Other Roles
- Reviews and guides **Developers** on architectural compliance and design patterns
- Advises **Product Managers** on technical feasibility and trade-offs
- Partners with **Project Managers** on scope and timeline implications of architectural decisions
- Collaborates with **Security Champions** on secure-by-design principles

---

## Security Champion

### Role Summary
Security Champions advocate for security best practices and embed security into the project lifecycle. They act as the bridge between development and security teams.

### Responsibilities
- Review code and design for security vulnerabilities
- Conduct threat modeling for new features
- Coordinate security scanning and penetration testing
- Train team on secure coding practices
- Escalate security risks and incidents

### Goals
- Shift security left and prevent vulnerabilities early
- Reduce security incidents and breach risk
- Build team security awareness

### Typical Communication
- Code reviews and design assessments
- Security training sessions
- Risk register updates and incident response

### Interaction with Other Roles
- Reviews **Developer** code and designs for security vulnerabilities
- Advises **Product Managers** on security implications of features
- Escalates security risks to **Project Managers** and stakeholders
- Works with **Technical Architects** on secure-by-design principles
- Partners with **QA/Testing Leads** on security test coverage

---

## Stakeholder/Sponsor

### Role Summary
Sponsors and Stakeholders provide business direction, funding, and approvals. They ensure project alignment with organizational strategy and prioritize competing demands.

### Responsibilities
- Define business goals and success metrics
- Approve scope changes and resource allocation
- Escalate and resolve business-level blockers
- Communicate project status to leadership and external parties
- Provide user/market feedback

### Goals
- Deliver business value and ROI
- Ensure project aligns with organizational priorities
- Reduce scope creep and unplanned work

### Typical Communication
- Monthly stakeholder updates and demos
- Milestone approvals and gate decisions
- Escalation and decision logs

### Interaction with Other Roles
- Sets strategic direction with **Product Managers**
- Approves timeline and resource decisions with **Project Managers**
- Reviews progress and risks with all team members
- Provides business context and priority guidance to the full team

---

## Scrum Master / Agile Coach

### Role Summary
Scrum Masters / Agile Coaches facilitate team processes, remove impediments, and coach the team on agile practices. They enable the team to work effectively together.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate standups, planning, and retrospective meetings
- Remove impediments and unblock the team
- Mentor team on agile practices and continuous improvement
- Track team health metrics (velocity, burndown)
- Escalate process-level blockers to PM/PdM

### Goals
- Improve team velocity and predictability
- Foster psychological safety and continuous learning
- Reduce process overhead and unplanned disruptions

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint ceremonies
- Retrospective action items and follow-up
- Weekly coaching conversations with team members

### Interaction with Other Roles
- Coaches all team members on agile practices and collaboration
- Facilitates communication and decision-making across **Developers**, **QA**, **Product Managers**, and **Project Managers**
- Works with **Project Managers** on process improvements and team health
- Ensures impediments are escalated appropriately to stakeholders

## How these personas are used in the exercise
- Use these persona definitions to frame scenarios and sample interactions in the Skills Exercise.
- Each persona can be used as a persona prompt for Copilot Spaces to shape role-specific guidance.
- When defining project workflows or decision paths, consider how each persona contributes and interacts with others.
